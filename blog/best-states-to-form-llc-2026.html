Best States to Form an LLC in 2026

Compare Delaware, Wyoming, Nevada, Texas & Florida — costs, taxes, and benefits

Choosing the best state to form an LLC in 2026 is one of the most important decisions you'll make as a new business owner. The state you pick affects your filing costs, annual fees, tax obligations, and the level of legal protection you receive. While many entrepreneurs default to their home state, others look to business-friendly jurisdictions like Delaware, Wyoming, or Nevada for extra advantages.

In this guide, we'll compare the five most popular states for LLC formation — Delaware, Wyoming, Nevada, Texas, and Florida — so you can make an informed decision based on your specific situation.

1. Wyoming — Best Overall for Small Businesses

Wyoming has earned its reputation as the best state to form an LLC for most small business owners. With the lowest combined costs in the nation and strong privacy protections, it's hard to beat.

Pros

  • Lowest ongoing costs: Just $100 to file and $60/year to maintain — the cheapest in the country.
  • No state income tax: Wyoming has zero personal or corporate income tax.
  • Strong privacy: Member and manager names aren't required on public filings.
  • Lifetime proxy: Allows a nominee to act on behalf of members for added privacy.
  • Asset protection: Wyoming was the first state to create the LLC structure and has the strongest charging order protections.

Cons

  • If you operate in another state, you'll still need to register as a foreign LLC there.
  • Limited local market if you need a physical Wyoming presence.

2. Delaware — Best for Startups Seeking Investors

Delaware is the gold standard for venture-backed startups and larger businesses. Over 60% of Fortune 500 companies are incorporated here, and investors are familiar with its well-established legal framework.

Pros

  • Business-friendly courts: The Court of Chancery specializes in business disputes with experienced judges (no juries).
  • Investor confidence: VCs and angel investors prefer Delaware entities.
  • Flexible LLC laws: Delaware's LLC Act allows extensive customization of operating agreements.
  • Low filing fee: Only $90 to form your LLC.

Cons

  • $300 annual franchise tax regardless of revenue.
  • 8.7% corporate income tax if you have nexus in Delaware.
  • Overkill for most small businesses that don't plan to seek venture capital.

3. Nevada — Best for Asset Protection

Nevada markets itself aggressively as a tax haven, and it does offer some genuine advantages — but at a higher price point than competitors like Wyoming.

Pros

  • No state income tax: Zero personal, corporate, or franchise tax.
  • Strong charging order protection: Creditors cannot seize LLC membership interests.
  • Privacy: Operating agreement members aren't listed publicly.

Cons

  • High costs: $425 filing fee plus $350/year in annual fees — the most expensive on this list.
  • Commerce tax: Businesses grossing over $4 million pay a commerce tax.
  • Many of Nevada's benefits are matched by Wyoming at a fraction of the cost.

4. Texas — Best for Texas Residents

If you live and operate your business in Texas, forming your LLC in-state is almost always the right call. Texas has no personal income tax and no annual report requirement.

Pros

  • No personal income tax: Texas is one of nine states with no income tax.
  • No annual report: Unlike most states, Texas doesn't require annual filings.
  • Massive local economy: Access to one of the largest markets in the U.S.

Cons

  • Franchise tax: LLCs with revenue over $2.47 million owe a franchise (margin) tax.
  • $300 filing fee: Higher than Wyoming or Delaware.
  • Public information requirements — member/manager names are on file.

5. Florida — Best for E-Commerce and Service Businesses

Florida combines zero state income tax with reasonable filing costs, making it a strong choice for online businesses, freelancers, and service-based companies.

Pros

  • No personal income tax: Great for pass-through LLC taxation.
  • Affordable fees: $125 to file, about $139/year to maintain.
  • Growing business ecosystem: Particularly strong for tech, real estate, and e-commerce.

Cons

  • 5.5% corporate income tax applies if you elect corporate taxation.
  • Annual report is required every year by May 1.
  • Less privacy than Wyoming — managing members are listed publicly.

Should You Form in Your Home State?

Here's the truth most "best state" articles won't tell you: if you're a small business operating in one state, form your LLC there. Forming in Wyoming or Delaware when you operate in California means you'll pay fees in both states — the "cheap" out-of-state LLC becomes expensive fast.

💡 Rule of thumb: Form in your home state unless (1) you have no physical presence in any state (e-commerce, digital nomad), (2) you're seeking venture capital (Delaware), or (3) privacy is your top priority (Wyoming).

Bottom Line: Which State Is Best for You?

  • Most small businesses: Form in your home state — check our state-by-state guides.
  • Location-independent businesses: Wyoming offers the best value.
  • Startup seeking investors: Delaware is the standard.
  • Maximum asset protection on a budget: Wyoming beats Nevada at half the cost.
  • Already in Texas or Florida: Stay in-state — Texas and Florida are already excellent.
